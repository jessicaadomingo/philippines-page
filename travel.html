Travel

The decision to travel to the Philippines is an easy one. The country has a lot to offer in terms of nature, food, and history. Many travelers come to witness its beaches, lagoons, and smaller islands. Below are some travel suggestions for anyone contemplating on visiting.

Luzon

Intramuros, Manila

The walled city of Intramuros (literally: "within walls") is historical gem, found within the city of Manila. Here you will find authentic remnants of Spanish colonization. During Spanish colonial times, this walled area was built as a military base. Today, it is preserved as an attraction to teach visitors about Spanish colonization, as well as to monument how the Manila looked like before the city was demolished from World War II.

Visayas

Kalanggaman Island, Leyte

Kalanggaman is a small island whose beach stretches out in a straight line, cutting through the ocean. Accessible by small boat, this island is a great day to spend the day and bask in the nature of the Philippines. Both shores are only meters apart, leaving you with the feeling of being deep within an ocean, while still remaining on land.

Mindinao

Mount Apo, Davao City

For the more adventurous and nature-loving, Davao City offers Mount Apo, the Philippines' tallest mountain. Local tourist guides lead 3 day long hikes up this mountain, whose summit sits at 9,692 ft above sea level.
